Sea Otter

Sea otters often hold paws while sleeping to prevent drifting away from one another.

  • Scientific Name: Enhydra lutris
  • Average Weight: 49-99 lbs for males; 31-73 lbs for females
  • Average Lifespan: 10-15 years for males; 15-20 years for females
  • Habitat: Northern & Eastern coasts of the North Pacific Ocean

The sea otter is an adorable mammal that lives in shallow waters off the coast of the North Pacific Ocean. Covered in thick, dense fur, the sea otter feeds on many of the sea's other inhabitants, such as urchins, mussels and even octupi. Sea otters are considered a keystone species, meaning their role in the ecosystem is vital to maintain a delicate balance. As such, it is vital for the continued conservation and reintroduction of this species.
